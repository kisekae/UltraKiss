KiSS Audio Types

The original KiSS specification implemented audio capabilities using sound files and music files.  Sound files were implicitly associated with streaming audio and music files were intended for sequencer type audio files. This was the typical technology to support audio in the early days. Therefore, sound files are traditionally Microsoft WAV files and Sun AU files, and music files are traditionally MIDI files.

Today, MP3 sound is standard for music. Many other audio formats also exist, and even within some formats such as WAV different encodings can be used. FKiSS 5 capable viewers should offer support for MP3 files and other popular formats and provide improved capabilities for managing audio playback. 

Regretfully, there is no standard way to ensure audio compatibility across different systems. 

The ability to play different sounds is a basic feature of a KiSS viewer. Music and sound are separate entities and playback of music files is distinct from sound files.

There is no official designation as to whether multiple sound or music files should be played concurrently or separately. Many KiSS viewers will play sounds concurrently. The sound("") and music("") commands with no file name specified are signals to stop all sound and music playback instantly.

;[FKiSS Section]
;@EventHandler

; Sound tests
; -----------

;@press(#7) ;
;@ sound("spacemusic.au") ;
;@press(#8) ;
;@ sound("22-new.aif") ;
;@press(#9) ;
;@ sound("sfx-medley.rmf") ;
;@press(#10) ;
;@ sound("trippygaia1.mid") ;
;@press(#11) ;
;@ sound("Bass Test - 100hz.mp3") ;
;@press(#12) ;
;@ sound("1-welcome.wav") ;

; Music tests

;@press(#21) ;

Compatibility:

WAV, AU, and MIDI file formats should be supported by all KISS viewers  FKiSS 5 viewers should implement MP3 sound and music playback. Support for other audio formats such as RMF, AIFF, and others is viewer dependent.

 

FKiSS 5 extensions:

FKiSS 5 introduces the mediastart() and mediastop() events to facilitate control of media playback. Basic FKiSS is deficient in that sound playback can be initiated through the sound() or music() action command, but no process exists to recognize when such files stopped or started.

FKiSS 5 extends the syntax of sound() and music() action commands to allow for the specification of an optional repeat count on the audio playback.
